Lychee Fundraiser and Arduinos




As you may know, we are hosting the lychee fundraiser not only to raise funds for the club but also to help people who want to buy Arduinos fund their purchase. The little infographic I made above should explain how the process works and how it helps us save money.

Anyway, tax included, the Arduino Uno Rev3 comes to about $24. Our awesome treasurer, Stella Saw, collected money for the lychee, but not the Arduino yet, so if you're planning to buy one, please message Stella on Facebook to let her know that you're interested in being part of the group order, and she'll tell you how much money to bring to the meeting this Thursday, April 19.

Special JPL Tour




Thanks to Lan Dang, a software engineer at Pasadena’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory, eight Coding Club members had the opportunity to go on a special JPL tour on Monday, 3/19. They met the creator of the Mars Images app, Dr. Mark Powell, who described how he built the app and imparted some programing advice. The tour group also watched scientists working on parts of a rover in a clean room, heard the story about the JPL lucky peanuts, learned about working and getting a job at JPL from David, and saw the Charles Elachi Mission Control Center, home to the “Center of the Universe”, while listening to an astronomer turned engineer's talk about working at JPL.

Code Day LA





A big thank you to everyone who attended Code Day LA!
Code Day LA is a 24-hour event anyone can attend to improve their coding skills and have fun.
Held at Toolbox, the event featured a basic coding lesson for beginners, a League of Legends tournament, delicious food, helpful volunteers and lots of awesome participants. In addition, there was a nationwide Code Cup competition which Los Angeles placed fourth in.
The event organizers also hosted a general coding competition with prizes including MVP, Best Presentation, Best Game, Best Application and Best Overall.



Congratulations to Coding Club member Aditya Bahawal for winning Best Presentation with his fun Unity game, Disco Snake.



Also, the game Hard Wrap, created by officers Sandra Tang and Stella Saw as well as Arcadia High alumni David Tang, won Best Game. Play the game here.
